Middlebury is the shiretown of Addison County, home to Middlebury College, with a population hovering around 8,000.
It was founded in 1763, originally granted by the governor of the New Hampshire colony, Benning Wentworth.
It is well-known for its beauty and has been featured in many films and TV shows.
The town is nestled at the base of the Green Mountains and is home to one of the
last remaining Ben and Jerry's© shops in the country.

From 1921-1962, acclaimed poet Robert Frost spent every summer and fall at his house in the nearby town of Ripton.
He taught at Middlebury College's mountain campus, the Bread Loaf School of English. He is credited to have had a major influence on the development of the school and its English Department.
